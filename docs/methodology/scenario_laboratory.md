# Scenario Laboratory

## Purpose

The Scenario Laboratory is the decision-support and exploration component of EPIDEM.

It allows users to create, store, compare, export, import, and interpret alternative epidemiological scenarios using a common modelling framework.

The objective is not forecasting but structured comparison of assumptions and outcomes.

---

## Core principles

The Scenario Laboratory is based on:

- transparency of assumptions;
- reproducibility;
- side-by-side comparison;
- educational value;
- planning support.

---

## Scenario definition

A scenario is a complete set of simulation parameters.

Examples include:

- baseline transmission;
- mobility changes;
- containment strategies;
- demographic assumptions;
- disease severity assumptions.

Each scenario can be saved and reused.

---

## Comparison framework

Scenarios are evaluated using common output indicators.

Examples include:

- peak active infections;
- peak timing;
- cumulative infections;
- cumulative recoveries;
- cumulative deaths;
- geographic dissemination;
- affected countries.

---

## Interpretation support

The platform provides interpretation support by translating quantitative differences into structured scenario summaries.

Interpretation is intended to facilitate understanding rather than provide recommendations.

---

## Export and import

Scenarios can be exported and imported to support:

- teaching exercises;
- collaborative work;
- reproducible analyses;
- demonstration sessions.

---

## Educational use

The Scenario Laboratory is designed to help users understand how assumptions influence model behaviour.

It should be interpreted as a learning and exploration environment.

---

## Planning use

The Scenario Laboratory may be used for planning exercises and structured discussion of alternative assumptions.

Results should not be interpreted as forecasts of future events.

---

## Future development

Planned developments include:

- multi-scenario comparison dashboards;
- scenario cards;
- scenario versioning;
- scenario sharing;
- enhanced reporting;
- situational awareness integration.
