# EPIDEM v0.2.0 – Country Expansion, Land Mobility and Pathogen Presets

## Objective

The objective of v0.2.0 is to improve the realism of geographic dissemination scenarios by:

- expanding country coverage;
- incorporating land-border mobility;
- introducing pathogen presets;
- preserving computational efficiency and transparency.

This development is intended to support educational, research and planning scenarios involving regional dissemination patterns that may not be adequately represented by air travel alone.

---

# Motivation

The current implementation relies primarily on international passenger mobility.

This approach is appropriate for many globally disseminated respiratory scenarios but may underestimate geographic spread in situations where:

- neighbouring countries have strong land-border connectivity;
- informal crossings are common;
- local population movement dominates international air travel.

Examples include recurrent Ebola outbreaks in Central and East Africa.

# Land Mobility Module

## Concept

A second mobility layer will complement the existing air passenger matrix.

Current:

Mobility_total = Air_Mobility

Future:

Mobility_total =
    Air_Mobility +
    Land_Mobility

---

## Structure

New object:

land_border_mobility_matrix

Dimensions:

Country x Country

---

## Initial implementation

The first implementation should remain simple:

Land_Mobility =
    Border_Adjacency
    × Crossing_Intensity
    × Mobility_Modifier

Where:

- Border_Adjacency = 0 or 1
- Crossing_Intensity = relative crossing weight
- Mobility_Modifier = scenario-specific adjustment

---

## Advantages

- computationally efficient;
- transparent;
- easy to explain;
- compatible with existing architecture.

---

## Future extensions

Potential future developments:

- regional mobility layers;
- seasonal mobility;
- refugee movement scenarios;
- dynamic border restrictions.

# Computational Impact Assessment

## Current architecture

Current simulations operate efficiently using country-level matrices.

## Expected impact

Approximate matrix sizes:

50 countries:
- 2,500 connections

100 countries:
- 10,000 connections

150 countries:
- 22,500 connections

These sizes remain feasible within the current architecture.

---

## Main bottlenecks

Expected bottlenecks are more likely to occur in:

- map rendering;
- visual updates;
- scenario comparison dashboards;

rather than in transmission calculations.

# Pathogen Presets

## Objective

Introduce predefined epidemiological profiles.

These presets provide starting assumptions for educational and planning scenarios.

---

## Initial preset categories

- Ebola-like scenario
- COVID-like scenario
- Influenza-like scenario
- Measles-like scenario

---

## Preset contents

Each preset may include:

- transmission parameters;
- exposed duration;
- infectious duration;
- severity assumptions;
- mortality assumptions;
- mobility sensitivity;
- containment sensitivity;
- documentation references.

---

## User interaction

Users may:

- select a preset;
- modify parameters;
- save custom variants.

Presets should never lock parameter values.
