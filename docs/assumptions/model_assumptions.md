# EPIDEM Model Assumptions

## Scope

EPIDEM is a population-level epidemiological simulation platform.

The model represents aggregated populations and country-level dynamics. It does not simulate individuals, households, workplaces, contact networks, or specific transmission chains.

---

## Compartment definitions

### Susceptible (S)

Individuals who are not currently infected and may become infected if exposed to transmission pressure.

### Exposed (E)

Individuals who have been infected but are not yet infectious.

The exposed compartment is used only in SEIRD configurations and represents a delay between infection and infectiousness.

### Infectious (I)

Individuals capable of contributing to transmission.

The infectious compartment represents aggregated transmission pressure rather than observable clinical cases.

### Recovered (R)

Individuals who have left the infectious compartment and are assumed not to contribute further to transmission.

### Deceased (D)

Individuals who have died as a consequence of the simulated disease process.

---

## Reproduction parameters

### Basic reproduction number (R0)

R0 represents the expected number of secondary infections generated by an infectious individual under baseline conditions.

### Effective reproduction number (Rt)

Rt represents transmission under the current simulated conditions.

Rt may vary according to containment measures, behavioural assumptions, mobility effects, and other scenario parameters.

---

## Mobility

International spread is represented through aggregated passenger flows.

Mobility does not represent physical transmission mechanisms. Instead, it provides a population-level approximation of opportunities for geographic dissemination.

Passenger volumes are used as a proxy for connectivity between countries.

---

## Demographic adjustment

Country-specific age distributions can be incorporated into the model.

Age structure is used to adjust severity and mortality assumptions while preserving computational efficiency.

The age-adjusted implementation is intended to improve realism without moving to individual-based simulation.

---

## Containment measures

Containment measures are represented through parameter modifications affecting:

- transmission intensity;
- mobility intensity;
- timing of spread.

Measures are model abstractions and should not be interpreted as exact representations of real-world interventions.

---

## Comparator framework

Reference simulations can be loaded from fixed RDS files.

The current implementation includes a COVID-19 Omicron comparator used for educational and benchmarking purposes.

Comparators are intended to facilitate relative scenario interpretation rather than direct forecasting.

---

## Interpretation of outputs

Simulation outputs should be interpreted as:

- scenario exploration;
- comparative analysis;
- educational demonstrations;
- planning exercises.

Outputs should not be interpreted as forecasts, predictions, or estimates of future real-world events.

---

## What the model does not attempt to represent

The model does not explicitly represent:

- individual behaviour;
- contact tracing;
- healthcare workflows;
- diagnostic testing systems;
- pathogen evolution mechanisms;
- within-host processes;
- clinical decision-making;
- healthcare resource allocation at patient level.

These simplifications are intentional and support computational efficiency and transparency.

---

## Known limitations

Results depend on:

- model assumptions;
- parameter choices;
- demographic inputs;
- mobility assumptions;
- reference datasets.

All outputs should be interpreted within these limitations.
